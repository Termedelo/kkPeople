The Engine Room

The Full Recruitment Cycle

A complete guide from start to finish. Hover over cards for tips.

01

Client Request

Deep dive into the JD & Pain Points.

Pro Tip

Don't just read the JD. Ask: "What business problem does this hire solve?"

02

Sourcing

The Hunt: LinkedIn, Behance, Referrals.

Pro Tip

Great candidates aren't applying. They are waiting for you to find them.

03

Outreach

Personalized messaging via Sourcing Platforms.

Pro Tip

Mention a specific detail from their profile in the first sentence to increase reply rates.

04

CV Review

Initial filter against the brief.

Pro Tip

Look for career progression and gaps. Red flags are just questions waiting to be asked.

05

Screening

The most critical quality gate.

Pro Tip

Listen more than you talk. If you speak for more than 40% of the call, you failed the screen.

06

Submission

Sending the candidate to the client.

Pro Tip

Highlight the 'Why' - match their skills explicitly to the client's pain points.

8-9

Interview Loop

Managing 1st, 2nd, & 3rd rounds.

Pro Tip

Speed kills deals. Get client feedback within 24 hours of every interview.

10

The Offer

Negotiation & Acceptance.

Pro Tip

Closing starts at the first call. Know their salary expectations and notice period from Day 1.

Guardians of the Brand

Reputation Management

We don't just fill roles; we build legacies. Every interaction shapes how the market sees us, our clients, and our candidates.

The KK Standard

We are experts, not spammers. We represent quality and integrity.

  • No "Spray & Pray". We target with precision.
  • Honesty over commissions. If a role isn't right, say so.
  • We are consultants, not order takers.

The Client's Voice

To the candidate, YOU are the company. Don't damage their brand.

  • Know their values before you pitch their role.
  • A bad interview process reflects poorly on the client.
  • Protect their confidentiality at all costs.

Candidate Respect

They are people, not products. Today's candidate is tomorrow's client.

  • Zero Ghosting Policy.
  • Provide constructive feedback, even if it's a "No".
  • Respect their time. Be punctual.
